What's Happened

This website was created in the aftermath of Covid-19. With all the free time afforded by the stay at home order, I decided to compile my travel experiences into one place. It was also a good chance to brush up on my web development skills, so I went all in. I started working on it in late October of 2020, so if you're seeing an unpolished site, just give it some time. All the trips I've taken are in a blog format on the blog page, but if you're interested in a specific type of trip, I have links that filter the blog posts below. Enjoy!

What's Happening

I'm currently in the process of bringing all of the functionality I had planned for the website into reality. I'm always working on ways to improve the user interface or ways to make the blogs more interactive. If you have any suggestions please contact me through the link in the header on every page. As time goes on you will see a more polished site that truly embodies my love of travel, but in the meantime enjoy what is available. The blogs I do have posted are all personal accounts of some of my favorite trips. You can view them on a map or just view each post separately. If you would like to have one of your own posts featured, please send in an email containing your name, age, and documents you would like included in the blog post (word document, pictures, etc.).

What Will Happen

In the months to come, I will be learning to fly a powered paraglider. I'll be joining the paramotor community. I'm excited to see the world from a whole different point of view. I am also taking a huge trip to Iceland in June. I am hoping I can find a way to fly through Iceland and make some amazing memories from the skies. It will be my first trip outside of North America, so I'm really excited already. In the future I also plan to compile travel stories from public figures and people I look up to. Eventually, I'd love to start planning trips for others too, and have a section where people can pick an area and enjoy the trip without too much planning on their end.
